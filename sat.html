The real problem

Your school built the wrong
instinct for the SAT

CBSE and ICSE spend 12 years training one cognitive mode: see a question, scan for the familiar answer, reproduce what you know. That mode is exactly what the SAT punishes. The College Board builds every wrong answer specifically to catch the student who is doing that. This is not a content problem. It is a thinking problem. And thinking can be retrained.

🔍
What your school trained: seeing for You look at a question and scan for the answer that matches — the familiar phrase, the word from the passage, the pattern you have seen before. Fast. Confident. Wrong on the SAT's hardest questions.
🎯
What the SAT rewards: seeing into You look through the question — past its surface, past your instinct — and hold its meaning separate from its words. Then you find the one answer that is defensible from that meaning alone. Not creative. Not clever. Precise.
🪤
Why every wrong answer is a trap, not a mistake The College Board designs wrong answers to feel correct to a student using the wrong cognitive mode. The True-but-Irrelevant trap. The Same-Words-Different-Logic trap. The Too-Extreme trap. Once you can name them, you can see them being set — and walk around them.
✅
Study-Flow: daily mirrors until the switch is complete Every morning question shows you which mode you used. Every nightly explanation names the trap, shows what seeing into looks like, and tells you what to watch for tomorrow. After 8 weeks of this — 96 daily mirrors — test day is Question Drop number 97. Familiar. Manageable. A cakewalk.

Common questions

Everything you need to know

Who is this programme for?

Indian students (Grades 10–12), NRI students, and international school students targeting US, UK, or other global university admissions. CBSE, ICSE, IGCSE, and IB students are all welcome — the Day 1 diagnostic identifies exactly which cognitive habits your curriculum built and where the work needs to happen. Both complete beginners and students who have done prior prep benefit — because most prior prep addressed content, not the underlying thinking pattern.

What score can I expect?

That depends on your starting point and consistency — and we will be honest with you about both on Day 1. Students who do the daily questions, submit their mistake log, and attend Friday Zooms consistently typically see 150–250 point improvements. More importantly, the goal is not the highest possible score — it is the score that opens the right university for you specifically. That conversation begins on Day 1 and runs through the entire 8 weeks.

Do I need to buy any books or materials?

No. Everything is included. The only thing you need is the free Bluebook app (from College Board) for mock tests. All question materials, explanations, and resources are delivered via WhatsApp and the Zoom sessions.

What if I miss a day's question?

You won't lose access — all questions and explanations stay in the WhatsApp group. But consistency is the whole point. Students who skip more than 3 consecutive days without communicating typically don't see the results they want. If you know you'll miss a stretch (exams, travel), let us know and we'll adjust your follow-up schedule.

Is the Friday Zoom recorded?

Yes. Every Zoom session is recorded and the link is shared in the WhatsApp group within 24 hours. We recommend attending live for the doubt session (you can ask follow-up questions), but the recording is available if you're unavailable.

What is the digital SAT format?

The SAT has been fully digital since March 2024. It's 2 hours and 14 minutes, 98 questions total, across two sections: Reading & Writing (54 questions) and Math (44 questions). Each section has two adaptive modules — your Module 1 performance determines the difficulty of your Module 2. Scores are returned in 2–3 days. This new format is central to what we teach.

How is this different from Khan Academy?

Khan Academy is excellent free content — but it's self-directed. There's no accountability, no personalised follow-up, no live sessions, no community, and no one who knows your specific mistake patterns. Study-Flow is a structured system with daily touchpoints and a human layer. Most of our students have already used Khan Academy before joining us.

Why only 10 students?

Because the core of this programme is personal visibility. Every mistake log is read before every session. Every student's cognitive map is tracked weekly. Every Thinking Audit in the Friday Zoom requires genuine individual attention. Beyond 10–12 students, that quality of attention is not possible to maintain honestly. This is not a marketing limit. It is an operational one.

Is there a refund policy?

Attend the first two Zoom sessions, complete the first 14 daily questions, and if the programme is not right for you, contact us within Day 14 for a full refund. No questions asked. We ask for two weeks because the cognitive shift takes time to become visible — students who quit after Day 3 have not yet seen what the system produces. Give it a genuine two-week try. If it is not working, we will return every rupee.
