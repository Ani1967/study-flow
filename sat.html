Common questions

Everything you need to know

Who is this programme for?

Indian students (Grades 10–12) and NRI / international school students targeting US university admissions. Both complete beginners (no SAT knowledge) and students who have done some prep are welcome — we have two tracks and the diagnostic on Day 1 assigns you correctly.

What score can I expect?

Results depend on your starting point, consistency, and how many sessions you attend. Students who do the daily questions and attend Friday Zooms consistently typically see 150–250 point improvements over 8 weeks. Our standard target is 1400+. Students starting below 1000 work toward 1200–1300 in the first cohort.

Do I need to buy any books or materials?

No. Everything is included. The only thing you need is the free Bluebook app (from College Board) for mock tests. All question materials, explanations, and resources are delivered via WhatsApp and the Zoom sessions.

What if I miss a day's question?

You won't lose access — all questions and explanations stay in the WhatsApp group. But consistency is the whole point. Students who skip more than 3 consecutive days without communicating typically don't see the results they want. If you know you'll miss a stretch (exams, travel), let us know and we'll adjust your follow-up schedule.

Is the Friday Zoom recorded?

Yes. Every Zoom session is recorded and the link is shared in the WhatsApp group within 24 hours. We recommend attending live for the doubt session (you can ask follow-up questions), but the recording is available if you're unavailable.

What is the digital SAT format?

The SAT has been fully digital since March 2024. It's 2 hours and 14 minutes, 98 questions total, across two sections: Reading & Writing (54 questions) and Math (44 questions). Each section has two adaptive modules — your Module 1 performance determines the difficulty of your Module 2. Scores are returned in 2–3 days. This new format is central to what we teach.

How is this different from Khan Academy?

Khan Academy is excellent free content — but it's self-directed. There's no accountability, no personalised follow-up, no live sessions, no community, and no one who knows your specific mistake patterns. Study-Flow is a structured system with daily touchpoints and a human layer. Most of our students have already used Khan Academy before joining us.

Is there a refund policy?

If you attend the first two Zoom sessions and complete the first 14 daily questions and feel the programme isn't right for you, contact us within Day 14 for a full refund. After Day 14, the enrolment is non-refundable. We stand behind the system — we just need you to give it a genuine two-week try.
